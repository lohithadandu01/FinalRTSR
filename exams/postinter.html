List of Some Common Entrance Tests in India for the Field of Engineering


  • Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main
  • Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Advanced
  • BITSAT (Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test)
  • VITEEE (VIT Engineering Entrance Examination)
  • Undergraduate Engineering Entrance Examination (UGEEE).
  • SRMJEEE (SRM Joint Engineering Entrance Examination)
  • Manipal Entrance Test (MET).
  • KIITEE (Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology Entrance Examination)
  • LPUNEST (Lovely Professional University National Entrance and Scholarship Test)



1.Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Main:-


Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), JEE Main is one of the most widely recognized entrance exams for admission to undergraduate engineering programs in India. It is the gateway to various National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and other centrally funded technical institutions.


2.Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Advanced:-


JEE Advanced is the next level of examination for admission to the prestigious Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). Only those candidates who qualify in JEE Main are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.


3.BITSAT (Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test):-


Conducted by BITS Pilani, BITSAT is an online entrance exam for admission to integrated first-degree programs offered at BITS campuses in Pilani, Goa, and Hyderabad.


4.VITEEE (VIT Engineering Entrance Examination):-


Conducted by Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT), VITEEE is an entrance exam for admission to undergraduate engineering programs at VIT campuses in Vellore, Chennai, Amaravati, and Bhopal.


5.SRMJEEE (SRM Joint Engineering Entrance Examination):-


SRMJEEE is conducted by SRM Institute of Science and Technology for admission to its engineering programs offered at campuses in Chennai, Amaravati, Delhi-NCR, and Sonepat.


6.Undergraduate Engineering Entrance Examination (UGEEE):-


UGEEE is conducted for admission to undergraduate engineering programs at the Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs) in India. The IIITs are a group of autonomous public technical universities in India that focus on information technology. They offer undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in various disciplines related to information technology and computer science.


7.Manipal Entrance Test (MET) :-


MET is an entrance examination conducted by Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE) for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs offered by the university. Formerly known as Manipal University, MAHE is a deemed university located in Manipal, Karnataka, India.


8.Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology Entrance Exam (KIITEE) :-


KIITEE is an entrance examination conducted by the Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT), Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India. KIIT is a deemed university offering a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in fields such as engineering, medicine, management, law, and more.


9.LPUNEST (Lovely Professional University National Entrance and Scholarship Test) :-


LPUNEST (Lovely Professional University National Entrance and Scholarship Test) for admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate programs offered by the university. LPUNEST is conducted multiple times a year, usually in different phases, to provide students with multiple opportunities to appear for the exam. The exam is held in online mode (computer-based test) at various centers across India. Additionally, LPU also offers the option for students to take the exam remotely from their own homes.


These are some of the common entrance tests in India for engineering. Each exam has its own eligibility criteria, syllabus, and examination pattern, so it's important for candidates to thoroughly prepare for the specific exams they plan to appear for. There are many more private colleges and universities that conduct entrance examinations for undergraduate admissions in various fields such as engineering, management, pharmacy, law, etc. It's essential for students to research and understand the specific requirements and procedures of each entrance exam and institution they are interested in applying to.



List of Some State-Level Engineering Entrance Exams in India



1.Andhra Pradesh Engineering, Agriculture and Medical Common Entrance Test (AP EAMCET):-


Conducted by the Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Kakinada on behalf of the Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education.

2.Telangana State Engineering, Agriculture and Medical Common Entrance Test (TS EAMCET):-


Conducted by Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Hyderabad on behalf of the Telangana State Council of Higher Education.

3.Karnataka Common Entrance Test (KCET) Conducted by the Karnataka Examination Authority (KEA):-


for admission to various undergraduate engineering courses in Karnataka.

4.Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT CET):-


Conducted by the State Common Entrance Test Cell, Maharashtra, for admission to engineering colleges in Maharashtra.

5.Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA):-


Conducted by Anna University, Chennai, for admission to engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu.

6.Uttar Pradesh State Entrance Examination (UPSEE)>:-


Conducted by Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Uttar Pradesh, for admission to engineering colleges in Uttar Pradesh.

7.West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination (WBJEE):-


Conducted by the West Bengal Joint Entrance Examinations Board for admission to engineering colleges in West Bengal.

8.Gujarat Common Entrance Test (GUJCET):-


Conducted by the Gujarat Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Board (GSEB) for admission to engineering colleges in Gujarat.

9.Rajasthan Engineering Admission Process (REAP):-


Conducted by the Rajasthan Technical University (RTU) for admission to engineering colleges in Rajasthan.

10.Haryana Common Engineering Entrance Test (Haryana CEET):-


Conducted by Haryana State Technical Education Society (HSTES) for admission to engineering colleges in Haryana.

11.Odisha Joint Entrance Examination (OJEE):-


Conducted by the Odisha Joint Entrance Examination Board for admission to engineering colleges in Odisha.

12.Madhya Pradesh Pre Engineering Test (MP PET):-


Conducted by the Madhya Pradesh Professional Examination Board (MPPEB) for admission to engineering colleges in Madhya Pradesh.

These are some of the prominent state-level engineering entrance exams in In>dia. Each state may have its own specific exam for admission to engineering colleges within the state.
