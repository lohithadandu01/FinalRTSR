EXAMS POST 10th


After completing the 10th standard, students have two main career options to pursue engineering:

1. Polytechnic/Diploma
2. Intermediate Education.

ENTRANCE EXAMS FOR POLYTECHNIC/DIPLOMA


Polytechnic entrance exams vary by region and institution but generally assess students' knowledge in subjects like mathematics, science, and general aptitude. The exam may also include sections on reasoning and English proficiency, depending on the specific requirements of the institution. The syllabus for these exams often aligns with the 10th standard curriculum, focusing on topics relevant to engineering disciplines. Here's a list of some top entrance exams for diploma courses after completing the 10th standard:

1.Joint Entrance Examination Polytechnic (JEE Polytechnic):-


Conducted by various state boards and technical education departments, JEE Polytechnic is a common entrance exam for admission into polytechnic/diploma courses in engineering and technology.

2.Polytechnic Common Entrance Test (POLYCET):-


POLYCET is conducted by state boards such as Telangana State Board of Technical Education and Training (TSBTET) and Andhra Pradesh State Board of Technical Education and Training (APSBTET) for admission into diploma courses in engineering and non-engineering fields.

3.Delhi Common Entrance Test (Delhi CET):-


Delhi CET is conducted by the Department of Training and Technical Education (DTTE), Delhi, for admission into diploma courses in engineering, technology, and other disciplines offered by polytechnic institutions in Delhi.

4.Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education (MSBTE) Entrance Exam:-


MSBTE conducts its own entrance exam for admission into diploma courses in engineering, pharmacy, and other fields offered by polytechnic institutions in Maharashtra.

5.DCET:-


DCET is conducted by the Karnataka Examination Authority (KEA) for lateral entry admission into the second year of diploma engineering courses in Karnataka.

6.West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination for Polytechnic (WBJEE-Polytechnic):-


WBJEE-Polytechnic is conducted by the West Bengal State Council of Technical and Vocational Education and Skill Development (WBSCTVESD) for admission into diploma courses in engineering and technology in West Bengal.

These exams are highly competitive, and success in them opens up opportunities for pursuing diploma courses in various technical fields, laying the foundation for a career in engineering and related industries. It's important for students to research and understand the eligibility criteria, exam pattern, syllabus, and application process for each exam they plan to appear for. Additionally, they should consider the reputation of the institution offering the diploma course and ensure that it aligns with their career goals in engineering.


ENTRANCE EXAMS FOR INTERMEDIATE


In India, after completing the 10th grade, students typically enroll in intermediate colleges for further education. Here are some common exams that students may need to take to get into intermediate colleges in India:

1.State Board Exams:-


Each state in India conducts its own board exams for Class 10, such as:
- Secondary School Certificate (SSC) exams in states like Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Goa.
- Secondary School Leaving Certificate (SSLC) exams in states like Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu.
- Board of Secondary Education (BSE) exams in states like Odisha and Bihar.
Performance in these exams may be a factor in admission to intermediate colleges.

2.Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) Class 10 Exams:-


CBSE is a national-level board in India that conducts Class 10 exams. Some intermediate colleges may consider CBSE Class 10 exam scores for admission.

3.Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE) Class 10 Exams:-


ICSE is another national-level board in India that conducts Class 10 exams. Scores from ICSE Class 10 exams may also be considered for admission to intermediate colleges.

4.Entrance Exams for Specialized Intermediate Colleges:-


Some intermediate colleges in India specialize in specific streams such as science, commerce, or arts. These colleges may conduct their own entrance exams for admission to their programs.

5.Merit-Based Admissions:-


In addition to entrance exams, some intermediate colleges may admit students based on their performance in the Class 10 board exams or other academic criteria. Students with high grades or marks may be eligible for direct admission without the need for additional entrance exams.

6.Private College Entrance Exams:-


Private intermediate colleges may have their own entrance exams for admission. These exams may assess students' knowledge and skills in subjects relevant to the chosen program or stream.

Students should research the admission requirements of the intermediate colleges they are interested in and prepare accordingly for any required exams. Additionally, they should consider factors such as the curriculum, faculty, facilities, and extracurricular opportunities offered by different colleges before making a decision.


COMPETITIVE EXAMS AFTER 10th


There are various scholarship exams, government exams as well as competitive exams after 10th. These exams after 10th help students gain the requisite financial assistance to pursue their higher education or get admission into their dream university right after 10th. Here are the top entrance and competitive exams after 10th:



  • National Science Talent Search Examination (NSTSE)
  • National Talent Search Examination (NTSE)
  • Kishore Vigyanik Protsahan Yojana (KVPY)
  • Assessment of Scholastic Tests through Technological Testing (ASSET)
  • Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (INMO)
  • International Maths Olympiad (IMO)
  • Science Olympiad Foundation (SOF) Exams
  • Indian National Olympiad (INO)/National Cyber Olympiad (NCO)
  • International English Olympiad (IEO)


    • 1.National Science Talent Search Examination (NSTSE):-


    Conducted by the Unified Council, the NSTSE exam assesses a student’s ability to solve mathematical questions and answer conceptual questions related to Science. Based on the CBSE syllabus, this exam is available for students right from 2nd standard till 12th class. The reason why it has become one of the popular exams after the 10th is that it provides skill-based feedback to every applicant, be it those studying MPC subjects or those with BiPC subjects, very comprehensively and gives monetary rewards accordingly.

    2.National Talent Search Examination (NTSE):-


    A prominent one in the long list of exams after the 10th, the National Talent Search Examination (NTSE) is a renowned scholarship exam conducted at the national level by the National Council of Education Research and Training (NCERT). Specifically organised for 10th-standard students, NTSE comprises two stages, first at the State/UT level and then second at the National Level. It witnesses more than 50 lakh applications out of which select 2,000 are given the scholarship.

    3.Kishore Vigyanik Protsahan Yojana (KVPY):-


    Designed as an ongoing National Program of Fellowship in the discipline of Basic Sciences, Kishore Vigyanik Protsahan Yojana is conducted and funded by the Department of Science and Technology, Government of India. This is amongst the most sought-after exams after the 10th and aims to select aspirants wanting to study basic sciences courses and explore the research domain in Science.

    4.Assessment of Scholastic Tests through Technological Testing (ASSET):-


    Amongst the lesser-known exams after the 10th, ASSET is termed as a skill-based assessment test which aims to evaluate a student’s conceptual knowledge and analyses a school’s performance at global, national and regional levels by providing effective insights and reports. It comprises many multiple-choice questions that evaluate a student’s understanding of their school syllabus thus moving away from rote learning. It is generally designed as per CBSE, ICSE, IGCSE and prominent state boards’ curricula and is organised for students of classes III to X.

    5.Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (INMO):-


    Conducted by the Mathematics Cell of the Homi Bhabha Centre for Science Education on the 3rd Sunday of January every year, INMO is an opportunity for those who want to pursue a career in Mathematics. It is the 3rd stage of the 6-stage mathematics exam and only those who clear the first 2 levels are eligible to appear for INMO. The reason why it has become one of the most opted exams after the 10th is that the applicants become eligible to study BSc Mathematics at Chennai Mathematical Institute. Further, those who apply for Bachelor of Statistics/Maths at the Indian Statistical Institute are directly called for an interview without taking the entrance test.

    6.Science Olympiad Foundation (SOF) Exams:-


    With the aim of nurturing budding talent in the fields of English, Science, Maths, etc, the foundation conducts 6 national level Olympiads for school students every year. Having affiliations with the ICAI and British Council, SOF Olympiad also offers scholarship programs like Scholarship of Excellence in English to meritorious students. From Accountancy and Logical Reasoning to concepts related to MPC subjects [Maths, Physics, and Chemistry], students are assessed on an array of topics depending on the olympiad they have applied for. Enlisted are the tests conducted by the foundation:-

    • National Science Olympiad
    • National Cyber Olympiad
    • International General Knowledge Olympiad
    • International English Olympiad
    • International Commerce Olympiad
    • International Mathematics Olympiad
